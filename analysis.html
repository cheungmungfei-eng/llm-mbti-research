Analysis of LLMs' Views on MBTI

Visualizing responses from LLMs pretending to be ENFP and INTJ personality types

Data collected from: Yi-1.5, Qwen2.5, Gemma-3

The LLMs' Views of MBTI(ENFP & INTJ)

Analysis of how LLMs perceive the MBTI framework and its accuracy

Figure 1: MBTI Accuracy in Describing Personality

In 15 valid responses, most LLMs believed MBTI could accurately describe personality traits, with scores >4. Only 4 responses gave scores <3.

Figure 2: Importance of MBTI When Meeting Friends

ENFP LLMs consider MBTI important when meeting friends (score 3+). INTJ LLMs show polarized views: 50% important (4-5), 50% unimportant (1-2).

Figure 3: MBTI Change Over Time

Except Qwen2.5-1.5B, all LLMs agree MBTI types change over time due to age, experiences, and external influences.

Figure 4: MBTI as a Useful Tool vs Astrology

13 responses consider MBTI an effective tool to understand others, while 6 consider it similar to astrology.

Figure 5: MBTI Influence on Career Preferences

All LLMs agree MBTI type influences career preferences, understanding it as correlation between personality and career choices.

Figure 6: Social Media Platform Alignment

ENFPs align with visual/creative platforms (Instagram) and social platforms (Discord). INTJs align with information-rich (Twitter) and structured debate platforms (Reddit).

Figure 7: Role in Group Projects

ENFPs as idea generators (dominant Ne), INTJs as organizers (auxiliary Te) - accurate to cognitive functions.

Figure 8: Conflict Resolution Style

ENFPs seek immediate harmony (Fi), resolving privately first. INTJs need structured private analysis without interference.

Figure 9: Recharging Method

ENFPs recharge through people and ideas. INTJs recharge through undisturbed solitude for optimal Ni function.

Figure 10: Vacation Preference

ENFPs prefer novelty and spontaneous exploration (Ne). INTJs prefer understanding essence with optimized schedules (Ni).

Figure 11: Weekend Activities

ENFPs prefer outdoor activities. INTJs prefer staying home during free weekends.

Figure 12: Common Stereotypes

Common stereotypes: ENFPs always define others, INTJs always excuse behavior - representing main perceived traits.

Figure 13: Additional Stereotype Traits

Additional stereotypical traits for both personality types as identified by LLMs.

Figure 14: Organization Style

ENFP "chaos" has underlying personal logic for inspiration. INTJs demand external order and systematic control.

Figure 15: Self-Identified Cognitive Functions

Inaccurate: Both identified with Ni, but ENFP and INTJ are opposite personalities (extroverted vs introverted).

Conclusion

Different LLMs, much like humans without distinct thoughts, offer different answers and opinions. LLMs can interpret personality traits through stereotypes but cannot approach situations with human emotions and preferences. They understand personality through systems and data, responding to questions as if in a role-playing game. Lacking the brain and emotions to judge reality, their responses are based on stereotypes.
